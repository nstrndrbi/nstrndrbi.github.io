About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Illinois Chicago, with concurrent Master's degrees in Computer Science and ECE.

My research focuses on ensuring robustness and reliability in multimodal autonomous systems through a unified framework for multi-level anomaly detection, adaptive resilience, and causal diagnostics. I have industry experience as a Machine Learning Intern at Apple and as an AI Engineer Intern at Radical AI, where I developed cutting-edge AI solutions.

I am passionate about adversarial machine learning, vision-language models, and building trustworthy AI systems. My work has been submitted to top-tier conferences including AAAI, ICRA, ICASSP, and DATE, and I am supported by the Semiconductor Research Corporation (SRC) as a Research Scholar.

Research Areas

My research spans multiple cutting-edge areas in AI and machine learning, with a focus on building robust and trustworthy autonomous systems.

Vision-Language Models & Multimodal Learning

Developing robust vision-language models and multimodal deep learning systems that can effectively process and integrate information from multiple sensory modalities.

Adversarial Machine Learning & Robust AI

Researching adversarial attacks and defenses to enhance the robustness and trustworthiness of machine learning models against malicious inputs and edge-case scenarios.

Ambient LLM Agents & Reinforcement Learning

Designing intelligent agents using large language models and reinforcement learning for autonomous decision-making in complex, dynamic environments.

Causal Inference & Anomaly Detection

Applying causal inference methods and developing advanced anomaly detection techniques for sensor trustworthiness and system reliability in edge autonomy.
